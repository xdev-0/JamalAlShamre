The Islamic Caliphate States

The most important countries and regions ruled by the Islamic Caliphate throughout history

شبه الجزيرة العربية

The leader : Abu Bakr Al-Siddiq
The Founder: ﷺ The Messenger Muhammad
Capital : Al Medina
Location : Hijaz (west of the Arabian Peninsula) .
The Period: 632-661 CE
Cause of fall : The transfer of the caliphate to the Umayyads

The heart of the Islamic world and the starting point of the call, it was ruled by the Rightly-Guided Caliphs after the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, and witnessed the expansion of the first Islamic state .

The Levant

The leader : Khalid ibn al-Walid
Capital : Damascus
Location :The Levant Region
The Period : 634-750 CE
Reason for Fall:The Abbasid Revolution

One of the most important regions of Islamic conquests during the era of the Rashidun Caliphs, later becoming the center of the Umayyad Caliphate.

The Umayyad Dynasty

The Leader: Muawiya ibn Abi Sufyan
Founder: Muawiya ibn Abi Sufyan
Capital: Damascus
Location: The Levant
Period: 661-750 CE
Reason for Fall: The Abbasid Revolution

The first hereditary caliphate in Islamic history, significantly expanding the boundaries of the Islamic state.

Baghdad

The Leader: Abu Jafar al-Mansur
Founder: Abu al-Abbas al-Saffah
Capital: Baghdad
Location: Iraq
Period: 750-1258 CE
Reason for Fall: Mongol Invasion

Capital of the Abbasid Caliphate and center of science and culture during Islam's Golden Age, renowned for its House of Wisdom and magnificent libraries.

Cairo

The Leader: Jawhar al-Siqilli
Founder: Al-Muizz li-Din Allah al-Fatimi
Capital: Cairo
Location: Egypt
Period: 969-1171 CE
Reason for Fall: Saladin

Capital of the Fatimid Caliphate which established Al-Azhar and became a center of learning and knowledge in the Islamic world.

Istanbul

The Leader: Mehmed the Conqueror
Founder: Osman I
Capital: Istanbul
Location: Turkey
Period: 1453-1924 CE
Reason for Fall: Abolition of the Caliphate

Capital of the Ottoman Caliphate and the last capital of the Islamic Caliphate, serving as the center of Islamic rule for over 400 years.

Anatolia

The Leader: Orhan Ghazi
Founder: Osman I
Capital: Bursa
Location: Turkey
Period: 1299-1453 CE
Reason for Fall: Capital moved to Istanbul

The birthplace of the Ottoman Empire and the beginning of its expansion in Asia Minor before the conquest of Constantinople.

The Balkans

The Leader: Suleiman the Magnificent
Capital: Belgrade
Location: Southeastern Europe
Period: 1362-1913 CE
Reason for Fall: Balkan Wars

One of the most important regions of Ottoman expansion in Europe, witnessing many major historical battles.

The Arab Levant

The Leader: Selim I
Capital: Damascus
Location: The Levant
Period: 1516-1918 CE
Reason for Fall: World War I

Encompassing Syria, Lebanon, Palestine, and Jordan, it served as an important administrative center in the Ottoman Empire.
